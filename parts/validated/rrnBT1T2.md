rrnBT1T2 — the tandem rrnB T1+T2 intrinsic (Rho-independent) double terminator from the E. coli rrnB ribosomal RNA operon; 217 bp, 100% identity to J01695 (positions 6362–6578).

## Origin
rrnBT1T2 is the complete T1-T2 transcription terminator cluster located downstream of the 5S rRNA gene in the *Escherichia coli* rrnB ribosomal RNA operon. The 217 bp are taken at 100% identity from the canonical rrnB operon sequence J01695 (positions 6362–6578, 1-based), first sequenced by Brosius et al. 1981. It is a composite of two atomic terminators: bp 1–169 are the T1 terminator, and bp 170–217 are the 3' portion of the T2 terminator region. The composite sequence is sourced directly from the native deposit, preserving the authentic T1–T2 junction, rather than assembled by concatenation.

## Properties
Both T1 and T2 are intrinsic (Rho-independent) terminators: each is a GC-rich RNA hairpin followed by a uridine tract that releases RNA polymerase without accessory factors. The T1 hairpin is followed by an unusually short 4-nt U-tract; NusA enhances T1 termination in vitro to in-vivo-equivalent levels (Schmidt & Chamberlin 1987). T1 alone terminates host (sigma-70) RNA polymerase at >97% efficiency in systematic flow-cytometry measurements (Cambray et al. 2013), placing it above the conventional >95% strong-terminator threshold. T2 carries a 10-bp palindromic stem and a longer U-tract; it terminates efficiently and shows reduced activity when inverted, consistent with hairpin dependence (Orosz et al. 1991). The two terminators behave as a single coupled cluster: only phage lambda N and Q antiterminators read through both together (Ghosh et al. 1991). The T1 element additionally carries a class II conserved sequence (TATCTGTT) that terminates phage (T7/SP6) RNA polymerases independently of the hairpin (Lyakhov et al. 1997; Sohn & Kang 2004).

## Use
The tandem arrangement provides strong, robust transcriptional insulation and is widely deployed as the downstream terminator of *E. coli* expression vectors (Brosius 1984). As a composite it complements the catalog's atomic T1 and T2 terminators: use rrnBT1T2 when a single strong double-terminator unit is wanted, or the individual atoms when finer control over a single terminator is needed. Both terminators act on *E. coli* RNA polymerase; the T1 class II signal extends termination to phage RNA polymerases.
