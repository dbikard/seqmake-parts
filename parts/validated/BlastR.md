BSD (blasticidin-S deaminase) is a 130-aa, zinc-dependent cytidine-deaminase-family enzyme from *Aspergillus terreus* that confers resistance to the antibiotic blasticidin S and is widely used as a dominant selectable marker.

## Origin
Cloned from *Aspergillus terreus* cDNA by Kimura et al. 1994 ([PMID 8159161](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8159161/)), who introduced BSD as a selectable marker for *Schizosaccharomyces pombe* and *Pyricularia oryzae*. Canonical entry: UniProt P0C2P0 (BSD_ASPTE), 130 aa.

## Properties
A single-domain enzyme of the cytidine/deoxycytidylate deaminase family (Pfam PF00383). It detoxifies blasticidin S by hydrolytically deaminating the cytosinine moiety to a non-toxic deaminohydroxy product. Catalysis uses a single catalytic zinc coordinated by three cysteines (Cys54, Cys88, Cys91) plus a water molecule, with Glu56 acting as the active-site proton donor; mutation of Glu56 or Cys91 abolishes activity ([PMID 10833262](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10833262/), [PMID 17959604](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17959604/)). The C54-A-E56 and PCGRC (PCxxC) motifs form the catalytic core. Substrate is held in an active-site pocket (Ser28, Arg82, Tyr126, Trp128), and a flexible C-terminal region (Tyr126-Gly130) governs substrate access ([PMID 11779186](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11779186/)). The enzyme is a homotetramer whose active sites are completed across subunits.

## Use
Dominant selectable marker conferring blasticidin S resistance across bacteria, yeast, fungi, and mammalian cells. As a CDS part it is protein-canonical (UniProt:P0C2P0); express from an appropriate promoter/RBS for the host.
