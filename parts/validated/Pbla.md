Pbla (P3): the constitutive sigma-70 promoter of the beta-lactamase (bla / AmpR) gene from pBR322; 70 bp.

## Origin
This 70-bp element is the beta-lactamase promoter region of plasmid pBR322, taken at 100% identity from the canonical reference sequence J01749. The promoter lies on the minus strand relative to conventional pBR322 numbering because it drives bla transcription in the opposite direction. The underlying nucleotide sequence of the bla region was first reported by Sutcliffe (1978).

## Properties
Pbla is a constitutive sigma-70 promoter with no known cognate transcriptional regulator. DNase I footprinting and in vitro transcription mapped its core elements and transcription start site (Russell & Bennett 1981), and S1 nuclease mapping of in vivo and in vitro RNA precisely located the start site about 35 bp upstream of the bla ATG, defining this as promoter P3 of the bla gene (Brosius et al. 1982). The promoter carries a -35 hexamer TTCAAA (4/6 match to the TTGACA consensus) and a -10 hexamer GACAAT (the 6-mer core of the GACAATA Pribnow box), separated by an optimal 17-bp spacer. Both hexamers are suboptimal relative to the sigma-70 consensus, consistent with weak-to-moderate constitutive output; a single base-pair substitution upstream is sufficient to create stronger overlapping promoters (Chen & Clowes 1984), underscoring that the wild-type elements are not consensus.

## Use
Use Pbla as a compact, regulator-free constitutive promoter to drive low-to-moderate expression in *E. coli*, for example to maintain a selection marker or housekeeping expression where tunability is not required. Its weak strength suits contexts where high-level transcription would be burdensome. When composing it into a construct, preserve its orientation (minus strand of the source plasmid) so transcription proceeds toward the downstream coding sequence.
