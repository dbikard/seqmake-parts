rrnBT1: the E. coli rrnB operon T1 intrinsic (Rho-independent) transcription terminator; 169 bp, >97% termination efficiency for host RNA polymerase.

## Origin
This 169-bp element is the T1 terminator from the T1-T2 terminator cluster located downstream of the 5S rRNA gene in the *Escherichia coli* rrnB ribosomal RNA operon. It is taken at 100% identity over its full length from the canonical rrnB operon reference sequence J01695, the operon first sequenced by Brosius et al. (1981). T1 is the first (5'-proximal) of the two tandem terminators in this cluster.

## Properties
rrnBT1 is a strong intrinsic, Rho-independent terminator: a GC-rich RNA hairpin (transcript 5'-CAGUCGAAAGACUGGGCCUUUCG-3') is followed by a short uridine tract that destabilizes the RNA:DNA hybrid and releases the polymerase. Lyakhov et al. (1997) resolved two distinct signals within T1 — a class I, hairpin-dependent signal and a class II conserved sequence (TATCTGTT) that is used only by phage (T7/SP6) RNA polymerases and operates independently of the hairpin — in the context of phage T7 RNA polymerase; Schwartz, Rahmouni & Boudvillain (2003) anatomized intrinsic-terminator hairpin contacts by interference analysis more generally, and Sohn & Kang (2004) characterized the class II conserved sequence mechanistically. The hairpin's role in host (sigma-70) RNA polymerase termination at this exact stem is inferred from the canonical intrinsic-terminator architecture rather than directly mapped. The U-tract here is unusually short (four uridines versus the typical six to eight); accordingly, Schmidt & Chamberlin (1987) found that NusA enhances T1 termination in vitro to levels matching in-vivo estimates. Even so, T1 is an efficient standalone terminator in reporter assays (Orosz et al. 1991), and a systematic measurement placed its termination efficiency above 97% (Cambray et al. 2013).

## Use
Use rrnBT1 to terminate transcription by *E. coli* (sigma-70) RNA polymerase — for example to insulate a downstream element from read-through, to cap an expression cassette, or to reduce transcriptional crosstalk in a circuit. It is frequently deployed together with its tandem partner T2 as a double terminator for stronger, more directional insulation. Place it on the same strand as, and immediately 3' of, the transcript to be terminated. The class II TATCTGTT signal additionally makes T1 an effective terminator for T7 and SP6 RNA polymerases, which is relevant when terminating phage-polymerase transcription.
