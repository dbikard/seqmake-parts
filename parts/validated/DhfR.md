Type I (DfrA1) trimethoprim-resistant dihydrofolate reductase from transposon Tn7 — a single FolA-fold catalytic domain (UniProt P00382), here carrying the M1V and N73D variants.

## Origin
DhfR is the type I dihydrofolate reductase (DfrA1, gene *dhfrI*/*dfrA1*) first sequenced from the trimethoprim-resistance region of transposon Tn7 ([PMID 6308574](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6308574/)). It is the canonical UniProt entry P00382 (DYR1_ECOLX), a 157-aa enzyme (EC 1.5.1.3). The catalogued construct matches P00382 with two substitutions, M1V (Val start) and N73D.

## Properties
The part is one FolA-fold catalytic domain co-extensive with the ORF (InterPro IPR001796, Pfam PF00186). It reduces dihydrofolate to tetrahydrofolate using NADPH. The active site is built from a glycine-rich loop (GNGPDIPW) that runs into helix B (PROSITE PS00075 conserved site), a substrate/cofactor loop equivalent to the E. coli FolA Met20 loop, and a hydrophobic inhibitor pocket. Trimethoprim resistance arises from a conserved pair of residues — a catalytic Glu (equivalent to Asp27 of chromosomal FolA) that H-bonds the diaminopyrimidine ring, plus an adjacent Gln that adds an extra H-bond to the inhibitor — together with antifolate-contacting residues Phe, Met, and Leu lining the pocket ([PMID 35562546](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35562546/); [PMID 27624966](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27624966/)).

## Use
DfrA1 is one of the most prevalent *dfr* cassettes on class 1/2 integrons and is widely used as a trimethoprim-resistance selection marker. As a part it provides trimethoprim (Tmp) selection in cloning and a well-characterized antifolate-target enzyme.
