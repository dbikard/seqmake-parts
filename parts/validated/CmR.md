CmR is the 219-aa type I chloramphenicol acetyltransferase (CAT) from E. coli transposon Tn9, conferring chloramphenicol resistance by O-acetylating the antibiotic.

## Origin
The part is the type I *cat* cistron of *E. coli* transposon Tn9, the canonical chloramphenicol-resistance determinant, first sequenced by Alton & Vapnek 1979 ([PMID 390403](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/390403/)). Its 219-residue sequence is identical to UniProt P62577 (CAT_ECOLX).

## Properties
A single chloramphenicol acetyltransferase domain (Pfam PF00302; InterPro IPR001707/IPR023213) adopting the CAT left-handed beta-helix fold. The active enzyme is a homotrimer with the active site at each subunit interface ([PMID 3288984](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3288984/); [PMID 2187098](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2187098/)). Catalysis proceeds via a ternary-complex acetyl transfer from acetyl-CoA to the C3-hydroxyl of chloramphenicol. His193 is the general base that deprotonates the C3-OH (UniProt ACT_SITE 193; equivalent to His195 of CATIII). Ser146 stabilizes the oxyanion of the tetrahedral transition state ([PMID 2109633](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2109633/)), and Asp197 positions and tunes the tautomer/pKa of catalytic His193. The CATI structure rationalizing these residues was solved by Biswas et al. 2012 ([PMID 22294317](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22294317/)).

## Use
Standard selectable marker conferring chloramphenicol resistance in *E. coli* and other bacteria. As a coding part it pairs with a separate promoter/RBS (the cognate *cat* promoter is catalogued separately as Pcat). Identity is protein-canonical (UniProt:P62577).
