# VSVG_tag

11-aa epitope tag (YTDIEMNRLGK) from the cytoplasmic tail of Vesicular stomatitis Indiana virus (VSVind) glycoprotein (VSV-G), detected by the P5D4 monoclonal antibody; used for protein detection, immunoprecipitation, and affinity purification across diverse expression hosts.

## Origin

The tag corresponds to residues 501–511 of the VSVind San Juan strain glycoprotein (UniProt P03522, reviewed Swiss-Prot entry), the absolute C-terminus of the 511-aa mature glycoprotein. The P5D4 monoclonal antibody was raised by immunisation with a peptide spanning the 15 C-terminal residues (aa 497–511) of VSV-G; competition mapping placed the minimal epitope at the five carboxy-terminal amino acids, with the 11-aa tag routinely used in practice [PMID:3013626]. The biological role of the cytoplasmic tail in Golgi-to-plasma-membrane transport was characterised independently [PMID:6352053].

## Properties

The YTDIEMNRLGK sequence is recognised by the P5D4 antibody with high specificity for the Indiana serotype of VSV-G; reactivity against other vesiculovirus G proteins varies [PMID:30232190]. The tag has no internal functional sub-elements; it acts as a single continuous binding epitope. As a free 11-aa peptide appended to a heterologous protein, it lacks the adjacent transmembrane helix of the native glycoprotein and therefore does not confer membrane anchoring. The tag is suitable for C-terminal fusions as the epitope is at the natural C-terminus of VSV-G [PMID:3013626]. A systematic comparison of epitope tags in rat CNS neurons following in vivo gene transfer placed the VSV-G tag among a panel of tags that includes c-Myc, HA, and FLAG [PMID:16630044].

## Use

- Immunodetection (Western blot, immunofluorescence, immunoprecipitation) via anti-P5D4 antibodies.
- Affinity purification of tagged fusion proteins using P5D4-coupled resins.
- PCR-based C-terminal chromosomal tagging: validated as one of 10 short epitope tags for endogenous C-terminal tagging by PCR in *S. cerevisiae* [PMID:19243080].
- In vivo protein tracking in mammalian neurons and other cell types [PMID:16630044].
