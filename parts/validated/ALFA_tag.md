# ALFA_tag

A 13-amino-acid synthetic epitope tag (SRLEEELRRRLTE) designed to form a stable alpha-helical structure and recognized by the NbALFA nanobody with sub-nanomolar affinity (Kd = 26 pM).

## Origin

The ALFA tag and its cognate nanobody NbALFA were developed by rational design of a synthetic helical peptide followed by phage-display-based nanobody selection. The tag sequence SRLEEELRRRLTE was derived from first principles to adopt a stable alpha helix with defined surface chemistry. The crystal structure of the NbALFA–ALFA tag complex was deposited in the PDB as entry 6I2G (released 2019; [PMID 31562305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31562305/)). The full-length peptide in the crystal is PSRLEEELRRRLTEP (15 aa); the flanking prolines are crystallographic contacts and are not part of the functional tag.

## Properties

The ALFA tag adopts an alpha-helical conformation upon binding NbALFA. Its key biochemical properties are:

- **Binding affinity:** NbALFA binds the ALFA tag with Kd = 26 pM (among the tightest epitope-tag/binder affinities known) ([PMID 31562305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31562305/)).
- **Position independence:** Functions as N-terminal, C-terminal, or internal fusion without loss of NbALFA recognition ([PMID 31562305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31562305/)).
- **Size:** 13 aa (39 bp coding sequence), small enough for CRISPR-mediated knock-in without perturbing the tagged protein ([PMID 36497133](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36497133/)).
- **Purification:** A cold-elutable NbALFA variant (NbALFACE, Kd ~100 nM) enables gentle single-step affinity purification at 4°C in physiological buffer ([PMID 33718340](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33718340/)).
- **Photocontrol:** A photocaged NbALFA variant (ALFA-Pb) carrying an unnatural amino acid at Tyr42 reduces affinity to 3.2–7.9 µM in the dark and recovers ~183–346 pM affinity after 365 nm UV irradiation ([PMID 35411584](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35411584/)).

## Use

The ALFA tag supports a broad range of applications across diverse organisms:

- **Detection and imaging:** immunofluorescence, confocal, and super-resolution microscopy using NbALFA conjugated to fluorophores or fluorescent proteins ([PMID 31562305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31562305/)).
- **Affinity purification:** via NbALFA resin (standard) or NbALFACE resin (cold-elutable) ([PMID 33718340](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33718340/)).
- **CRISPR endogenous tagging:** validated in nematodes (C. elegans, P. pacificus) and mammalian cells with the ANGEL approach ([PMID 36497133](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36497133/), [PMID 40883491](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40883491/)).
- **Yeast applications:** the ALIBY toolkit demonstrates compatibility with S. cerevisiae imaging and biochemistry ([PMID 36200780](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36200780/)).
- **Proximity proteomics:** split ALFA tag/NbALFA-TurboID system in mycobacteria ([PMID 40196699](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40196699/)).
