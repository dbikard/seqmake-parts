ermAM (ermB / erm(B)) is the Tn1545 erythromycin-resistance rRNA adenine N6-methyltransferase: a 245-aa enzyme that dimethylates A2058 of 23S rRNA to confer MLS-B (macrolide-lincosamide-streptogramin B) resistance.

## Origin
First sequenced from the conjugative transposon Tn1545 of *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (Trieu-Cuot et al. 1990). The protein is the reviewed UniProt entry P21236 (ERM_STREE), 245 aa, identical to this sequence. The names ermAM and ermB/erm(B) are used interchangeably in the literature for this determinant.

## Properties
A class-I S-adenosyl-L-methionine (SAM)-dependent methyltransferase (EC 2.1.1.184; Pfam PF00398, InterPro IPR001737). The NMR structure of ErmAM (Yu et al. 1997; PDB 1YUB) shows a bilobal fold: an N-terminal catalytic domain (~residues 1-180) with a seven-stranded beta-sheet flanked by alpha-helices plus a small two-stranded beta-sheet (the Rossmann-like SAM-binding fold), and a C-terminal domain (~181-245) of four alpha-helices forming a novel 23S rRNA-binding module. The glycine-rich Motif I G-loop (GTGKG) binds the SAM cofactor; the Motif IV motif (SIPY here) sits in the catalytic pocket, where the conserved aromatic residue stabilizes the flipped target adenine — the one indispensable active-site residue in the ErmC' homolog (Maravic et al. 2003), with catalytic-motif mutations abolishing Erm(B) activity (Farrow et al. 2002).

## Use
Selectable marker conferring erythromycin / MLS-B resistance, widely used in Gram-positive hosts (streptococci, enterococci, staphylococci, clostridia) and on broad-host-range vectors. Mechanism: ribosome modification blocks macrolide, lincosamide and streptogramin B binding (Weisblum 1995).
