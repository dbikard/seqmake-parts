TetA is the pBR322 class C tetracycline efflux pump, a 396-aa, 12-helix MFS antiporter that confers tetracycline resistance by exporting a tetracycline-divalent-cation complex in exchange for a proton.

## Origin
Encoded by the *tetA* gene of plasmid pBR322 (UniProt P02981, TCR3_ECOLX; *E. coli*). The 396-aa ORF corresponds to the revised pBR322 tetracycline-resistance sequence ([PMID 6307828](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6307828/)). It is the class C member of the *tetA* family — ~78% identical to class A (Tn*1721*/RP1) and ~45% to class B (Tn*10*) TetA. In its native context it is transcribed from the divergent *tetR-tetA* promoter region and induced by tetracycline.

## Properties
A member of the major facilitator superfamily (MFS) with 12 transmembrane helices arranged as two pseudo-symmetric 6-TM bundles joined by a large central cytoplasmic loop; both N- and C-termini are cytoplasmic. The 12-TM topology was mapped by TetA-PhoA fusions ([PMID 1517220](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1517220/)) and corroborated on the homologous Tn*10* TetA ([PMID 2545678](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2545678/)). Three conserved aspartates — Asp17 (TM1), Asp86 (TM3) and Asp287 (TM9) — are critical for function: Asp->Ala substitution abolishes or strongly reduces resistance ([PMID 1517220](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1517220/)). The cytoplasmic TM2-TM3 loop carries the diagnostic MFS/tet-efflux Motif A (GxxxDRxGRR, here GALSDRFGRR), whose conserved Arg is required for transport ([PMID 14702405](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14702405/)). The four native cysteines (Cys59, Cys139, Cys178, Cys381) are important but not essential — a Cys-less protein retains substantial activity ([PMID 10049405](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10049405/)).

## Use
Standard selectable marker conferring tetracycline resistance in Gram-negative hosts; the historical resistance determinant of pBR322 and derivatives. Annotated here as a protein-canonical CDS (UniProt:P02981) so that the helix and functional-residue features can be projected onto any in-frame insertion of the gene.
