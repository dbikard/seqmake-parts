# Twin_Strep_tag

Twin-Strep-tag: two tandem Strep-tag II (WSHPQFEK) units joined by a GGGSGGGSGGS linker, giving bivalent high-avidity binding to engineered streptavidin for one-step affinity purification and detection.

## Origin
The Twin-Strep-tag was engineered by tandemly arranging two copies of the Strep-tag II octapeptide (WSHPQFEK) connected by a short, flexible glycine-serine linker (GGGSGGGSGGS), with an N-terminal Ser-Ala. The design builds directly on the Strep-tag II / engineered-streptavidin (Strep-Tactin) system, whose structural binding mode — a 3-10-helical conformation of WSHPQFEK in the biotin pocket of streptavidin — was defined crystallographically (PMID 8636976). Presenting two binding units lets a single tagged molecule engage two streptavidin subunits simultaneously, converting moderate single-site affinity into a strong avidity interaction (PMID 24012791). The canonical 30-residue sequence is corroborated structurally by co-crystallized Twin-Strep-tag peptide chains (PDB 6SOS / 6SOK; PMID 33639211).

## Properties
- Length: 30 aa; sequence SAWSHPQFEKGGGSGGGSGGSAWSHPQFEK.
- Architecture: Ser-Ala + Strep-tag II (residues 3-10, WSHPQFEK) + GGGSGGGSGGS linker (residues 11-21) + Strep-tag II (residues 22-30, WSHPQFEK).
- Binding: each WSHPQFEK unit contacts the engineered-streptavidin biotin pocket; the two units together bind bivalently, lowering the off-rate and yielding picomolar-range avidity — substantially tighter than the micromolar single Strep-tag II interaction.
- Elution is gentle and competitive (biotin or desthiobiotin), preserving native protein and complexes.
- Small, uncharged, and largely bioinert; tolerated at the N- or C-terminus and host-independent.
- A length-identical but distinct variant exists in patent filings (SAWSHPQFEKGGGSGGGSGGGSWSHPQFEK, a GGGS x3 linker); this record is the canonical product sequence with the GGGSGGGSGGS linker.

## Use
Used as a fusion tag for one-step affinity purification of recombinant proteins on engineered-streptavidin resins (Strep-Tactin / Strep-Tactin XT), for capture of intact multi-protein complexes, and for detection/immobilization. The high avidity supports purification directly from dilute sources such as cell-culture supernatants and stringent washing with low background, while mild competitive elution returns native, functional protein (PMID 24012791, 17571060, 25749948).
