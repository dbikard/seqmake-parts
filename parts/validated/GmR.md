GmR (aacC1 / AAC(3)-Ia) — gentamicin 3-N-acetyltransferase that confers gentamicin resistance by acetyl-CoA-dependent N-acetylation of aminoglycosides; full-length 177-aa match to UniProt P23181.

## Origin
The aacC1 gene was cloned and sequenced from R plasmids (R1033/Tn1696, R135) as an 833 bp cassette in Tn21-related transposons ([PMID 2549372](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2549372/)). The native N-terminus of the mature enzyme was determined by Edman sequencing ([PMID 668926](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/668926/)). The encoded protein is the canonical UniProt entry P23181 (AACC1_PSEAI, *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*), 177 aa.

## Properties
GmR is a single-domain member of the GCN5-related N-acetyltransferase (GNAT) superfamily. The catalytic core (residues 25-177) adopts the GNAT alpha/beta fold and contains the four conserved GNAT motifs (C, D, A, B), known from the homologous *Serratia marcescens* AAC(3) crystal structure ([PMID 9727487](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9727487/); PDB 1BO4) and reviewed by Vetting et al. ([PMID 15581578](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15581578/)). Motif A — the invariant Arg/Gln-X-X-Gly-X-Gly/Ala segment, here RRQGIA (residues 118-123) — hydrogen-bonds the acetyl-CoA pyrophosphate; the acidic motif B contributes the aminoglycoside-binding site.

## Use
GmR is a widely used selectable marker conferring resistance to gentamicin (and related aminoglycosides) by transferring an acetyl group from acetyl-CoA onto the 3-amino position of the antibiotic. It is the protein product of a selection cassette commonly carried in cloning and expression vectors.
