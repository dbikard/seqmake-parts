# SBP_tag

Streptavidin-binding peptide (SBP-Tag) — a 38-aa synthetic peptide that binds streptavidin with ~2.5 nM affinity for mild, single-step affinity purification and detection, eluted competitively with biotin.

## Origin
The SBP-Tag was derived from streptavidin-binding peptides isolated from a large random peptide library by mRNA display. The lead clone (SB19) bound streptavidin with a dissociation constant near 10 nM, and a deletion-refined derivative (SB19-C4) reached ~2.4 nM. From this lineage a defined 38-residue tag (MDEKTTGWRGGHVVEGLAGELEQLRARLEHHPQGQREP) was established for use as a recombinant-protein affinity tag (PMID 11274392; PMID 11722181). The sequence is verified to 100% identity over all 38 residues against the deposited co-crystal structure chain (PDB 4JO6).

## Properties
The peptide binds streptavidin with an equilibrium dissociation constant of about 2.5 nM. A 1.75 Å co-crystal structure shows it binds bivalently, occupying biotin-binding pockets on two separate streptavidin subunits: an N-terminal His-Val-Val motif (residues 12-14) engages one pocket and a C-terminal His-Pro-Gln motif (residues 31-33) the other, bridged by a central α-helix (residues 17-28) that projects three leucine side chains into a groove between protomers (PMID 23633599). Terminal residues 1-10 and 35-38 do not contact streptavidin; a minimal variant spanning residues 11-34 (SBP-Tag2) retains comparable affinity (PMID 23633599). At ~4 kDa the tag is small relative to protein fusion partners.

## Use
Genetic fusion of the tag to a recombinant protein enables single-step affinity capture on immobilised streptavidin, with very mild and specific elution by adding biotin to the wash buffer; the matrix capacity is about 0.5 mg of tagged protein per mL. Single-step purifications from bacterial extract yield preparations of higher purity than maltose-binding-protein or His-tag purification, suiting the tag to high-throughput workflows (PMID 11722181). The tag tolerates N-terminal, C-terminal, and internal placement and works across bacterial and mammalian systems. Beyond purification it supports fluorescence imaging with streptavidin conjugates and immunoblot detection, and is used to purify endogenous protein complexes; it also serves as a component of tandem affinity purification schemes for interaction proteomics (PMID 21194474; PMID 17060908; PMID 27924562).
