The p15A origin: a ~562 bp natural ColE1-type replication origin whose copy number is controlled by the divergent RNA I / RNA II antisense system.

## Origin
p15A is the replication origin of the *E. coli* plasmid p15A, the medium-copy origin used in the pACYC and pSU vector families. It was sequenced and functionally mapped by Selzer et al. 1983 ([PMID 6186390](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6186390/)), who showed primer (RNA II) transcription begins ~508 bp upstream of the replication start point and that the antisense RNA I (~105-108 nt) from the 294-524 bp upstream region governs incompatibility. It is a natural origin, not a synthetic promoter+operator construct.

## Properties
Replication is primed by RNA II, a pre-primer transcript made from a sigma70 promoter on the minus strand of this fragment (-35 box reading TTGAGA, -10 box reading TAATCT, canonical 17 bp spacer). After hybridizing to the template and being cleaved by RNase H, RNA II provides the free 3'-OH that DNA polymerase I extends. Copy number and plasmid incompatibility are set by RNA I, a short plasmid-specific antisense RNA transcribed from the opposite strand; RNA I binds the nascent RNA II and blocks productive primer formation (Tomizawa et al. 1981, [PMID 6165011](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6165011/)). The RNA I transcript ends at a rho-independent terminator (GC-rich inverted repeat CCGCCCTGCAAGGCGG followed by a poly-T tract) annotated near the 3' end of the part. Control is entirely RNA-based: there is no protein-DNA operator in this fragment, and the cognate Rop/Rom protein (an RNA-binding modulator) is not encoded here.

## Use
Standard medium-copy origin for *E. coli* cloning and expression vectors (pACYC184, pSU series and derivatives). Its incompatibility group differs from ColE1/pMB1 (pUC, pBR322) origins, so p15A-based plasmids can be co-maintained with ColE1-based plasmids in the same cell.
