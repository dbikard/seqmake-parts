# Myc epitope tag

Canonical 10 aa epitope tag (EQKLISEEDL) derived from human c-Myc proto-oncogene protein (UniProt P01106, residues 425–434), used for immunodetection and immunoprecipitation of recombinant fusion proteins via the 9E10 monoclonal antibody.

## Origin

The Myc epitope tag sequence EQKLISEEDL corresponds to residues 425–434 of the human c-Myc proto-oncogene protein (UniProt P01106, MYC_HUMAN, 454 aa, Swiss-Prot reviewed). The 9E10 monoclonal antibody was raised against synthetic peptides from human c-myc and shown to recognize this epitope on recombinant fusion proteins ([PMID:3915782](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3915782/)).

## Properties

The 9E10 antibody binds specifically to EQKLISEEDL; peptide spot synthesis and substitutional analysis established the minimum binding peptide as KLISEEDL and the core recognition motif as LISE ([PMID:11739900](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11739900/)). A key limitation is that 9E10 recognition is highly context-dependent: residues flanking the tag in the fusion protein can significantly reduce or abolish binding, particularly when EQKLISEEDL is embedded within structured regions ([PMID:31992583](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31992583/)). An evolved antibody variant (Hyper-Myc) with 18 nM affinity and improved thermal stability retains EQKLISEEDL specificity and may mitigate some of these context effects ([PMID:35312243](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35312243/)).

## Use

The Myc tag is fused genetically to a recombinant protein of interest (N-terminal, C-terminal, or internal) to enable immunodetection by Western blot, immunofluorescence, flow cytometry, or immunoprecipitation using anti-c-Myc antibodies that recognize EQKLISEEDL. Because the epitope is derived from a human protein, expression in mammalian systems can in principle elicit endogenous cross-reactivity; this is typically not an issue in non-human expression hosts. For sensitive or high-throughput applications, empirical testing of detection efficiency is recommended given the context-dependent nature of 9E10 binding.
