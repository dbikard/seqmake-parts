The pSC101 origin of replication: the ~220 bp low-copy, iteron-driven cis origin that controls RepA-initiated replication of the broad-host-range plasmid pSC101.

## Origin
pSC101 is a narrow-host-range, low-copy (~5 copies/cell) plasmid from *Salmonella*. Its minimal essential origin was defined as an ~220 bp region that requires the plasmid-encoded RepA (Rep) initiator supplied in trans ([PMID 8376344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8376344/)). This part corresponds to that cis origin region.

## Properties
The origin contains three tandem ~18 bp direct repeats (iterons) sharing a 17 bp core (TGTAAATTCTGCTAGAC), bound noncooperatively by the RepA initiator as a monomer; RepA binding bends the DNA helix axis (~100 degrees) ([PMID 12501319](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12501319/)). A host DnaA box sits at one end of the origin ([PMID 8376344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8376344/)), and an AT-rich tract (~82% AT) between the iterons and the DnaA box, together with the IHF box, sets up efficient Rep/IHF-induced bending ([PMID 12501319](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12501319/)). The region immediately downstream of the third iteron is required in a sequence-specific way for origin function ([PMID 8376344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8376344/)).

## Use
A standard low-copy origin for *E. coli* cloning and expression vectors; compatible with p15A-, ColE1-, and pBBR-based origins, which makes it a common choice for multi-plasmid systems ([PMID 2041467](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2041467/)). Copy number is tuned chiefly through the RepA initiator rather than this cis sequence.
