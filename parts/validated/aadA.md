aadA is the streptomycin/spectinomycin resistance marker - an aminoglycoside (3'')(9) adenylyltransferase, ANT(3'')(9) / ANT(3'')-Ia, EC 2.7.7.47.

## Origin
The classical Tn21-lineage aadA Sm/Sp resistance gene; its product is canonical AadA (UniProt P0AG05, 263 aa). This is the canonical enzyme: the 38-aa N-terminal cassette leader carried by the SEVA-distributed ORF ([PMID 23180763](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23180763/)) has been removed, leaving the resistance enzyme unchanged.

## Properties
AadA is a monomeric two-domain enzyme: an N-terminal polymerase-beta-like nucleotidyltransferase domain (Pfam PF01909) and a C-terminal alpha-helical bundle (Pfam PF13427 / DUF4111) ([PMID 26527143](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26527143/)). It adenylylates streptomycin (3''-OH) and spectinomycin (9-OH) using ATP-Mg2+, transferring AMP to inactivate the drug. Glu87 is the catalytic base; Asp47/Asp49 coordinate the catalytic Mg2+ ions in a two-metal-ion mechanism. ATP is bound by residues spanning both domains, including Asp130 (ribose) and Arg192/Lys205/Tyr231 (phosphates). Trp112 and Asp182 are shared streptomycin/spectinomycin contacts, while Trp173 and Asp178 are streptomycin-specific - W173A and D178A selectively reduce streptomycin but not spectinomycin resistance ([PMID 29871922](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29871922/)).

## Use
Selectable marker conferring streptomycin and spectinomycin resistance in Gram-negative hosts; a standard Sm/Sp resistance cassette (e.g. in SEVA-format plasmids).
