Pm is the XylS-dependent, benzoate/m-toluate-inducible promoter of the TOL (pWW0) plasmid meta-operon, here an 81-bp fragment carrying two tandem XylS operator repeats, a non-canonical -35, and an experimentally mapped -10 (TAGGCT).

## Origin
Pm is the promoter of the *meta*-cleavage operon of the Pseudomonas putida TOL plasmid pWW0. It is a natural fragment (not synthetic), classically captured in the broad-host-range pJB653/RK2 expression vectors and the GenBank record U75327.1 ([PMID 9097427](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9097427/)). Its biology and applications are reviewed in [PMID 28276630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28276630/).

## Properties
Activation requires the AraC/XylS-family regulator **XylS**, which is itself activated by benzoate/m-toluate effectors. The promoter region carries two imperfect 15-bp direct repeats matching 5'-TGCAN6GGNTA-3' (box A = TGCA, box B = GGNTA), separated by 6 bp: a distal repeat at ~-70/-56 (seq[10:25] = TGCAAGAAGCGGATA) and a proximal repeat at ~-49/-35 (seq[31:46] = TGCAAAAAATGGCTA) ([PMID 7969028](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7969028/)). XylS-CTD binds the two repeats sequentially and bends the DNA prior to activation ([PMID 20382764](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20382764/)). The proximal repeat's 3' end (TGGCTA, seq[40:46]) overlaps the -35; this XylS/RNAP overlap is essential for transcription ([PMID 12023029](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12023029/)). Pm lacks a canonical sigma-70 -35 and is transcribed with sigma-32 or sigma-38 depending on growth phase ([PMID 10096078](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10096078/)). The -10 element was experimentally determined as TAGGCT at -11/-6 (seq[67:73]) ([PMID 19201967](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19201967/)). All four sub-features reproduce exactly on the sense strand with no off-by-one.

## Use
Pm/XylS is a widely used tunable expression system for recombinant protein production and metabolic engineering in Gram-negative hosts, deployed in broad-host-range RK2-based vectors (pJB653 family). Expression is induced with benzoate/m-toluate; the regulator XylS must be supplied in cis or trans. The -10/spacer is a productive target for mutagenesis to tune output ([PMID 19201967](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19201967/)).
