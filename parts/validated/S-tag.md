# S-tag

15-aa N-terminal fragment of mature bovine pancreatic ribonuclease A (RNase A) that binds non-covalently to the complementary S-protein fragment with high affinity, reconstituting RNase activity; widely used as a protein affinity and quantitative detection tag.

## Origin

The biochemical basis of the S-tag system was established in 1959 when subtilisin cleavage of RNase A was found to yield two fragments — S-peptide (residues 1-20) and S-protein (residues 21-124) — that non-covalently reassemble to restore enzymatic activity (PMID:13654398). The S-tag sequence KETAAAKFERQHMDS corresponds to mature RNase A residues 1-15 (UniProt P61823, precursor positions 27-41). Truncation to 15 residues (S15) was shown to be sufficient for high-affinity S-protein binding and RNase activity reconstitution, and was developed as a genetically encodable fusion tag (PMID:8453373).

## Properties

- **Sequence**: KETAAAKFERQHMDS (15 aa; source: UniProt P61823, 100% identity)
- **Binding partner**: S-protein (RNase A residues 21-124); Kd ~10^-6 to 10^-7 M by calorimetry (PMID:2383573); functional reconstitution assays report Kd <= 10^-9 M (PMID:8453373)
- **Detection**: RNase activity-based detection reaches sensitivity >= 10^-16 mol (PMID:8453373)
- **D14N variant**: asparagine substitution at position 14 reduces S-protein binding affinity to allow nondenaturing elution; used for purification of acid-sensitive proteins (PMID:8453373)
- **Human ortholog variant**: a 15-aa S-tag derived from human RNase 1 retains the same architectural function with reduced immunogenicity for therapeutic applications (PMID:15914191)

## Use

The S-tag is appended to a protein of interest as an N- or C-terminal fusion. Affinity purification is achieved by capture on S-protein agarose resin followed by elution under denaturing or (for D14N variant) nondenaturing conditions (PMID:11036653). Quantitative detection exploits S-protein conjugates in an RNase activity assay. Dual-tag strategies combining S-tag with a second affinity handle allow sequential purification (PMID:11036653). The tag has been deployed in diverse recombinant expression systems including bacterial and mammalian cells.
