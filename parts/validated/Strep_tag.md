# Strep_tag

Strep-tag II is an engineered 8-residue synthetic affinity peptide (WSHPQFEK) that binds the biotin-binding pocket of streptavidin, enabling mild, highly specific single-step affinity purification and detection of recombinant proteins.

## Origin

The tag descends from a random-peptide-library selection for a short C-terminal peptide that binds streptavidin, which yielded the original Strep-tag (AWRHPQFGG) (Schmidt & Skerra 1993) and was first applied to one-step affinity purification on immobilised core streptavidin (Schmidt & Skerra 1994). Crystallographic analysis of the peptide:streptavidin complex (Schmidt et al. 1996) rationalised the binding mode and guided rational engineering to the second-generation Strep-tag II, WSHPQFEK, whose complex with streptavidin is deposited as PDB 1RSU at 1.70 Å. The peptide is purely synthetic and has no standalone natural protein of origin; the canonical sequence corresponds to positions 3–10 of PDB 1RSU chain P (the two N-terminal residues being an upstream cloning linker).

## Properties

- Sequence: WSHPQFEK (8 residues), molecule type protein.
- Mechanism: the peptide occupies the biotin-binding pocket of streptavidin in a 3-10-helical conformation; the Glu/Lys residues distinguish Strep-tag II from the first-generation Strep-tag and optimise this pocket-binding mode.
- Affinity: binds streptavidin with a dissociation constant of roughly 1 µM; affinity-engineered streptavidin variants (Strep-Tactin) raise the affinity by about an order of magnitude through stabilisation of a lid-like binding-site loop in an open conformation (Voss & Skerra 1997; Korndörfer & Skerra 2002).
- Elution is gentle and competitive — desthiobiotin or biotin under physiological buffer conditions — avoiding harsh denaturants.
- Small and minimally perturbing; functions at either the N- or C-terminus and across diverse expression hosts, from bacteria to mammalian cell culture (Skerra & Schmidt 1999, 2000; Schmidt & Skerra 2015).
- A tandem avidity variant, the Twin-Strep-tag, links two WSHPQFEK copies through a GGGSGGGSGGS linker to lower the off-rate and reach near-picomolar capture (Schmidt et al. 2013; Eichinger & Skerra 2021).

## Use

Fused at the N- or C-terminus of a protein coding sequence to enable affinity capture, purification, and detection on immobilised streptavidin or engineered streptavidin, with mild competitive elution that preserves activity and native complexes (Schmidt & Skerra 2007). Because capture is specific and elution is gentle, it is widely used for purifying intact protein complexes and has been embedded in engineered cell-surface receptors to enable identification, selection, and large-scale expansion of cells via streptavidin-bead capture (Liu et al. 2016).
