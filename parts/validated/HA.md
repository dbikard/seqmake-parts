# HA

Nine-residue epitope tag (YPYDVPDYA) derived from an immunodominant region of influenza A hemagglutinin, widely used for protein detection, immunoprecipitation, and affinity purification.

## Origin

The sequence YPYDVPDYA corresponds to residues 114–122 (1-based) of the influenza A virus (strain A/Aichi/2/1968 H3N2) hemagglutinin protein (UniProt P03437). This region was identified as an immunodominant antigenic determinant through peptide-scanning experiments and structural analysis [PMID:6204768]. The epitope was subsequently adopted as a general-purpose protein tag, first demonstrated by fusion to yeast adenylyl cyclase for affinity purification using the monoclonal antibody 12CA5 [PMID:2455217].

## Properties

The HA tag is a compact 9-amino-acid sequence (MW ~1.06 kDa) recognized with high affinity by well-characterized monoclonal antibodies, most notably 12CA5 (IgG2b) and 3F10 (IgG1 rat). The minimal size minimizes steric interference with most target proteins. A key limitation: the DVPD motif within the tag is a substrate for caspases 3 and 7; cleavage during apoptosis results in complete loss of immunoreactivity [PMID:17264856]. This must be considered in studies involving cell death or caspase activation.

## Use

The HA tag is incorporated as a short fusion at either the N- or C-terminus (or internally) of a protein of interest to enable detection by immunoblot, immunoprecipitation, immunofluorescence, and affinity purification. It functions across diverse expression systems including yeast, bacteria, insect cells, and mammalian cells. The tag is not suitable as a sole readout in apoptosis-related experiments due to caspase-mediated cleavage. Linker sequences are often added flanking the tag to reduce steric effects on the fusion partner.
