# FLAG

FLAG epitope tag (DYKDDDDK): an engineered hydrophilic 8-residue peptide used as an affinity and epitope tag, recognized by anti-FLAG M1/M2/M5 antibodies and N-terminally cleavable by enterokinase.

## Origin
FLAG is a synthetic, rationally designed marker peptide (Asp-Tyr-Lys-Asp-Asp-Asp-Asp-Lys) first described by Hopp and colleagues in 1988. It was engineered to be short, highly hydrophilic and antigenic so that it would sit at a protein surface, be readily recognized by antibodies, and carry a built-in enterokinase cleavage site for later removal. The sequence is source-verified at 100% identity over all 8 residues against the deposited FLAG-tag peptide in the anti-FLAG M2 Fab co-crystal structure (PDB 8RMO).

## Properties
- Length: 8 amino acids; net negatively charged and strongly hydrophilic.
- Recognized by the anti-FLAG monoclonal antibodies M1 (calcium-dependent, requires a free N-terminus), M2 (calcium-independent, position-insensitive) and M5. Detection and immunoaffinity capture are followed by either calcium chelation (M1) or peptide-competitive elution (M2/M5).
- The C-terminal Asp-Asp-Asp-Asp-Lys is an enterokinase (enteropeptidase) recognition site, so an N-terminal FLAG tag can be removed proteolytically.
- Atomic-resolution structure (1.16 Angstrom, PDB 8RMO) shows the peptide bound to the M2 Fab as a 3_10 helix, with D1, Y2, K3, D4 and D6 forming the antibody contacts. Independent mapping defines DYK(D)/DYKxxD as the minimal M2 epitope, with D5 and D7 dispensable.
- Engineering caveat: in the secretory pathway, Tyr2 can be sulfated by TPST2 (SLC35B2-dependent), which reduces anti-FLAG M2 and polyclonal recognition of secreted or membrane-displayed FLAG fusions. Background and proteolysis have also been reported in some insect-cell expression contexts.

## Use
FLAG is fused at the N- or C-terminus (or internally) of a target protein for one-step immunodetection (Western blot, immunofluorescence, immunoprecipitation) and immunoaffinity purification. Because it is a single functionally coherent 8-aa unit, it is treated as an atomic part. A higher-sensitivity tandem variant, 3xFLAG, embeds this epitope three times and is used as its own unit.
