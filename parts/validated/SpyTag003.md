# SpyTag003

SpyTag003 is a 16-amino-acid peptide (RGVPHIVMVDAYKRYK), the third-generation reactive Spy peptide, that forms a spontaneous, irreversible covalent (isopeptide) bond to its protein partner SpyCatcher003 at a rate approaching the diffusion limit.

## Origin
SpyTag003 descends from the SpyTag/SpyCatcher system, which was created by splitting the CnaB2 immunoglobulin-like adhesin domain of the fibronectin-binding protein FbaB from *Streptococcus pyogenes* - a domain stabilised by a spontaneous internal Lys-Asp isopeptide bond. Splitting CnaB2 gave the 13-residue SpyTag peptide and the 116-residue SpyCatcher protein, which re-associate and reform that bond between molecules ([Zakeri 2012](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1115485109)). Through library-based evolution and rational design the pair was accelerated stepwise (generations 001, 002, 003). SpyTag003 was obtained from SpyTag002 by appending an N-terminal Arg-Gly (RG) dipeptide - which adds positive charge complementing acidic residues on the Catcher - and introducing the T112H mutation (CnaB2 numbering); it is matched with SpyCatcher003 ([Keeble 2019](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1909653116)). The canonical sequence was deposited as protein QGX07220.1 / nucleotide MN433888.1.

## Properties
On simple mixing, SpyTag003's reactive Asp (CnaB2 Asp117) forms an intermolecular isopeptide (amide) bond to Lys31 of SpyCatcher003, catalysed by SpyCatcher Glu77 (an E77A mutation abolishes the reaction). The peptide contains no cysteine and the pair shows no detectable side reactions or self-reactivity. The second-order rate constant is 5.5 +/- 0.6 x 10^5 M^-1 s^-1 - about 400-fold faster than the original SpyTag/SpyCatcher (1.4 x 10^3 M^-1 s^-1) and 28-fold faster than SpyTag002/SpyCatcher002 - placing it within the 10^5-10^6 M^-1 s^-1 range taken as the onset of diffusion-controlled protein-protein interaction. Reaction half-time is under 30 s at 10 microM and under 5 min even with both partners at ~10 nM, reaching >90% completion at 10 nM in 15 min and >99% completion when one partner is in excess. The covalent reaction is effectively of infinite affinity; the docking step alone, measured on a reaction-dead Asp-to-Ala (DA) variant, has a noncovalent K<sub>d</sub> of 21 +/- 4 nM by surface plasmon resonance (kinetic estimates 3 and 13 nM) - too tight to measure by ITC ([Keeble 2019](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1909653116)).

## Use
SpyTag003 can be fused at the N-terminus, C-terminus or an internal position of a target protein (its engineered N-terminal RG extension is integral to its reactivity) and reacts rapidly and specifically with SpyCatcher003 across diverse contexts: *Escherichia coli*, human cells, and additional model-organism lysates. It enables fast, sensitive detection by flow cytometry and Western blotting, cell-surface labelling within 1 min on live mammalian cells, and intracellular reconstitution of split proteins (demonstrated by restoring talin function in the cytoplasm). Further applications include irreversible anchoring on beads or biomaterials, oligomerisation on plug-and-display vaccine scaffolds, and affinity purification of SpyTag003 fusions via an immobilised non-reactive SpyCatcher (Spy&Go / SpyDock), reported at ~92% purity ([Keeble 2019](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1909653116); [Khairil Anuar 2019](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1901876116)). Because the tag is inert alone, it is always deployed with its cognate SpyCatcher003; it is back-compatible with earlier SpyCatcher generations, and orthogonal covalent pairs (SnoopTag/SnoopCatcher, DogTag/DogCatcher) allow multi-way assembly.
