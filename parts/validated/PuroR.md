PuroR is the *Streptomyces alboniger* puromycin N-acetyltransferase (PAC), a 199-aa GNAT-family enzyme that inactivates puromycin by N-acetylation and serves as a dominant selectable marker.

## Origin
PuroR is encoded by the *pac* gene cloned from *Streptomyces alboniger* ([PMID 2676728](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2676728/)). Its use as a dominant selection marker derives from the demonstration that *pac* confers puromycin resistance when expressed in mammalian cells ([PMID 3714487](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3714487/)). The protein corresponds to UniProt P13249.

## Properties
PAC is a single-domain enzyme that adopts the GCN5-related N-acetyltransferase (GNAT) fold spanning essentially the entire chain (domain residues 6-198). The most distinctive structural feature is an alpha-helical hairpin inserted between beta-strands 3 and 4 (~residues 84-116) that forms the entry point for puromycin into the active site. Acetyl-CoA binds via a P-loop built on the conserved Q-X-X-G-X-G motif (QGKGLG, residues 135-140) after beta-strand 4, which coordinates the CoA pyrophosphate through backbone amides. Y171 is the likely catalytic general acid (Y171F greatly reduces activity), N167 contacts the CoA carbonyl and adenine, T77 contributes to puromycin binding, and E161 is a candidate distal general base ([PMID 33664348](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33664348/)).

## Use
Catalyzes transfer of an acetyl group from acetyl-CoA to puromycin, detoxifying the antibiotic and conferring puromycin resistance. Widely used as a dominant selectable marker for stable transfection/selection of mammalian cell lines.
