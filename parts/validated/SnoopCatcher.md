# SnoopCatcher

SnoopCatcher is a ~112-aa engineered protein partner, split from the D4 domain of the Streptococcus pneumoniae RrgA pilus adhesin, that forms a spontaneous, covalent, irreversible isopeptide bond with the peptide SnoopTag.

## Origin
SnoopCatcher derives from the D4 (Ig-like / CnaA-like) domain of RrgA, where a spontaneous intramolecular isopeptide bond naturally forms between Lys742 and Asn854. The domain was split in the loop following its N-terminal beta-strand to yield a short peptide (SnoopTag, RrgA 734-745) and a protein partner (SnoopCatcher, RrgA 749-860). To optimize the reaction the protein partner carries G842T (stabilizing a beta-strand) and D848G (stabilizing a hairpin turn near the reaction site). The stored sequence here is an exact match to a deposited SnoopCatcher G848D variant: it retains the G842T mutation but has Asp at position 848 (RrgA numbering; position 100 of the 112-aa domain), rather than the Gly of the canonical engineered form. The reactive Asn854 (position 106) is intact.

## Properties
On mixing with SnoopTag, SnoopCatcher reconstitutes the split domain and forms a spontaneous intermolecular isopeptide (amide) bond between its reactive Asn854 and the reactive Lys742 of SnoopTag, releasing ammonia; mutating Asn854 to Ala abolishes the reaction. The reaction reaches >99% yield with a modest excess of one partner, proceeds efficiently over pH 6-9 and 4-37 C, is insensitive to reducing agent (neither partner contains cysteine), and needs no specific buffer component (works in PBS, with mild detergents, or in high salt; modestly enhanced by trimethylamine N-oxide). The pair is orthogonal to SpyTag/SpyCatcher, with no detectable cross-reaction even overnight. SnoopCatcher expresses solubly in the E. coli cytosol.

## Use
SnoopCatcher captures any SnoopTagged protein into a covalent, SDS-stable conjugate, enabling irreversible protein-protein fusion without chemical modification. Combined orthogonally with SpyTag/SpyCatcher it supports sequential, programmable assembly of linear, branched, and combinatorial polyprotein chains ("polyproteams"), and it is used to display antigens modularly on multivalent nanoparticle and virus-like-particle scaffolds (plug-and-display). Note that this stored instance is the G848D variant; for the canonical engineered SnoopCatcher (D848G), use the corresponding wild-type form.
