# Spot_tag

12-aa affinity/epitope tag (PDRVRAVSHWSS) recognized with sub-nanomolar affinity by the BC2 nanobody; enables immunodetection, affinity purification, and super-resolution microscopy labeling across diverse cell types.

## Origin

Spot-tag is an affinity-optimized synthetic variant of the BC2-tag (PDRKAAVSHWQQ), which corresponds to residues 16–27 of human β-catenin (UniProt P35222). The BC2 epitope and its cognate VHH nanobody were first described by Braun et al. 2016 (PMID 26791954), who determined the crystal structure of the nanobody–peptide complex (PDB 5IVN) and established a "headlock" binding mechanism in which CDR3 Arg106 forms a salt bridge with Asp1 of the tag and packs tightly against the core of the peptide. The affinity-optimized Spot-tag variant (PDRVRAVSHWSS), differing from BC2-tag at four positions (K4V, A5R, Q11S, Q12S), was first publicly disclosed as SEQ ID NO:33 (pTag1) in patent US20170137467A1 (filed 2016-11-16, published 2017-05-18; Rothbauer et al., ChromoTek GmbH). The substitutions roughly double the affinity (KD ~0.7 nM for Spot-tag vs. ~1.4 nM for BC2-tag with the BC2 nanobody).

## Properties

- Sequence: PDRVRAVSHWSS (12 amino acids)
- Cognate binder: BC2 VHH nanobody; binding via headlock mechanism (CDR3 Arg106/Asp1 salt bridge; PDB 5IVN for BC2-tag complex)
- KD ~0.7 nM (Spot-tag/BC2 nanobody; patent US20170137467A1)
- Small, non-repetitive, and derived from a human protein sequence, minimizing steric perturbation and potential immunogenicity in mammalian systems
- Compatible with N-terminal, C-terminal, and internal fusion contexts

## Use

Spot-tag has been applied to:
- dSTORM single-molecule super-resolution microscopy of cytoskeletal and membrane proteins in mammalian cells and yeast, using a bivalent BC2 nanobody format site-specifically conjugated to organic fluorophores (Virant et al. 2018, PMID 29500346)
- Protein detection and localization in plant cells (*Arabidopsis thaliana*) via whole-mount protocols, demonstrated with the KIN7.3 microtubule motor (Mentzelopoulou et al. 2024, PMID 38779764)
- Affinity purification and pull-down via nanobody-conjugated resins (patent US20170137467A1)

The tag's small size (12 aa) is advantageous where larger fusion partners (e.g., fluorescent proteins) would interfere with target protein function or localization.
