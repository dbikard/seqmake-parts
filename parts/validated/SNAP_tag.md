# SNAP_tag

SNAP-tag is a ~182-amino-acid self-labeling protein tag engineered by directed evolution from human O6-alkylguanine-DNA alkyltransferase; it forms a covalent, irreversible bond with O6-benzylguanine (BG) substrates through its active-site cysteine, so a fusion protein is decorated with whatever synthetic probe is appended to the BG.

## Origin

SNAP-tag was generated by directed evolution of the human DNA-repair enzyme O6-alkylguanine-DNA alkyltransferase (hAGT, also called AGT or MGMT). Mutant libraries of hAGT were displayed on phage and selected for efficient reaction with the inhibitor O6-benzylguanine, based on their ability to irreversibly transfer the benzyl group to a reactive active-site cysteine ([PMID 12725859](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12725859/) | [doi:10.1016/S1074-5521(03)00068-1](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1074-5521(03)00068-1)). The founding method demonstrated that such an engineered tag enables covalent labeling of fusion proteins with small synthetic molecules in vivo, complementing earlier noncovalent strategies ([PMID 12469133](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12469133/) | [doi:10.1038/nbt765](https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt765)).

The stored 178-aa core corresponds exactly (100% identity) to the canonical SNAP-tag protein record (UniProt E5BBQ0, derived from a deposited crystal structure). The stored form has an unresolved N-terminal boundary discrepancy: it begins with the natural hAGT initiator methionine rather than the synthetic cloning prefix found in the deposited records, and is three residues shorter than the deposited 182-aa form. The exact source of this stored form is not yet verified and is flagged for human re-sourcing.

## Properties

- **Covalent, irreversible self-labeling:** the tag transfers the benzyl group of an O6-benzylguanine substrate onto its active-site cysteine, forming a stable covalent bond and releasing guanine; the probe attached to the benzyl group becomes permanently linked to the fusion protein ([PMID 12725859](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12725859/)).
- **Single catalytic cysteine:** activity depends on one active-site cysteine (canonically Cys145 in hAGT numbering; residue 144 in this stored sequence). Mutating this cysteine to alanine (C145A) abolishes alkyl-transfer activity ([PMID 9934856](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9934856/) | [doi:10.1093/carcin/20.1.103](https://doi.org/10.1093/carcin/20.1.103)).
- **Modular probe delivery:** a single genetic fusion can be decorated with many distinct functional groups (fluorophores, affinity handles, solid supports) simply by changing the molecule conjugated to the common O6-benzylguanine scaffold ([PMID 18291317](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18291317/) | [doi:10.1016/j.chembiol.2008.01.007](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chembiol.2008.01.007)).
- **Orthogonal companion tag:** the AGT-derived CLIP-tag reacts with O2-benzylcytosine substrates and is orthogonal to SNAP-tag, allowing two fusion proteins to be labeled simultaneously and specifically with different probes ([PMID 18291317](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18291317/)).
- **Engineerability:** the engineered variant SNAP-tag2 reaches a second-order labeling rate constant approaching 10^7 M^-1 s^-1 with rhodamine substrates (~100-fold faster than SNAP-tag) and is ~5-fold brighter with fluorogenic dyes ([PMID 40610720](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40610720/) | [doi:10.1038/s41589-025-01942-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41589-025-01942-z)).

## Use

- **Live-cell and in vitro protein labeling:** covalent attachment of synthetic fluorophores and other probes to SNAP-tag fusions for imaging and protein-function studies in living cells and in vitro ([PMID 12469133](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12469133/), [PMID 12725859](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12725859/)).
- **Fluorophore labeling and pulse-chase tracking:** specific labeling with synthetic fluorophores enables multicolor imaging and pulse-chase visualization of distinct protein generations in one sample ([PMID 18291317](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18291317/)).
- **Two-color orthogonal labeling:** paired with the orthogonal CLIP-tag for simultaneous, spectrally distinct labeling of two different fusion proteins ([PMID 18291317](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18291317/)).
- **Super-resolution microscopy:** used (and benchmarked against HaloTag) for STED/super-resolution imaging with cell-permeable fluorogenic BG substrates ([PMID 30745239](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30745239/) | [doi:10.1016/j.chembiol.2019.01.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chembiol.2019.01.003)).
