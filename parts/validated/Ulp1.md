# Ulp1

Ulp1 catalytic domain (residues 403-621 of S. cerevisiae Ulp1, UniProt Q02724): the SUMO/Smt3-specific cysteine protease used to remove N-terminal SUMO fusion tags and release a passenger with a native, scarless N-terminus.

## Origin
This part is the catalytic (UD/Ulp) domain of the Saccharomyces cerevisiae SUMO protease Ulp1, residues 403-621 of the 621-aa UniProt entry Q02724 (ULP1_YEAST), at 100% identity over the 219 aa. The 403-621 fragment is the canonical construct used for crystallography (the Ulp1-SUMO co-crystal, PDB 1EUV) and for recombinant expression. In its native context, the full-length enzyme also carries an N-terminal region (roughly residues 1-346) that directs nuclear-envelope/nuclear-pore localization and helps shape substrate specificity; that region is not part of this fragment.

## Properties
Ulp1 is a cysteine endopeptidase (clan CE; the SENP/Ulp family) that acts by a Cys-His-Asp catalytic triad. On this part the triad residues map to part positions 178 (Cys580, the nucleophile), 112 (His514), and 129 (Asp531) in full-length numbering; the assignment comes from the co-crystal structure and is supported by mutagenesis. The enzyme recognizes the folded three-dimensional structure of SUMO rather than a linear sequence motif, and cleaves immediately after the SUMO C-terminal Gly-Gly. Cleavage tolerates most residues at the P1' (+1) position with the notable exception of proline, so a product whose native N-terminus is proline cannot be released scarlessly. The isolated catalytic domain (from approximately residue 417) is sufficient for the essential SUMO-deconjugation function in vivo, and the first ~13 residues of the 403-621 fragment are dispensable for activity.

## Use
The purified catalytic domain is used as a high-activity protease to remove N-terminal SUMO solubility/expression tags, releasing the passenger protein with a native N-terminus; it has been reported to be active across a broad range of buffer and temperature conditions. The soluble 403-621 fragment can be toxic to E. coli host cells during production; an N-terminally truncated 416-621 form produced from inclusion bodies and refolded is an attested alternative. Solubility-enhanced engineered derivatives and a divergent thermophilic-fungal SUMO protease (with higher reported turnover) have also been developed from and alongside this wild-type construct. The cognate substrate is the SUMO/Smt3 tag, available as a separate catalog part.
