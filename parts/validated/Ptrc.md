Ptrc is the 30-bp core of the trc promoter, a synthetic trp/lac hybrid with perfect-consensus -35 and -10 boxes separated by a consensus-optimal 17-bp spacer.

## Origin
Ptrc is a synthetic hybrid promoter derived from the *trp* and *lac* promoters of *Escherichia coli*. It descends from the tac promoter (de Boer 1983, [PMID 6337371](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6337371/)), which fuses the *trp* -35 hexamer (perfect-consensus TTGACA) to the *lac* UV5 -10 hexamer (perfect-consensus TATAAT). trc differs from tac by a single base-pair insertion in the spacer, increasing the -35-to--10 spacing from 16 bp (tac) to the consensus-optimal 17 bp (trc) (Amann 1985, [PMID 3007288](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3007288/); Brosius 1985, [PMID 2579077](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2579077/)).

## Properties
- **-35 box** (positions 0-6, TTGACA): perfect-consensus -35 hexamer, *trp*-derived.
- **-35/-10 spacer** (positions 6-23, 17 bp): consensus-optimal spacing; the defining trc-vs-tac feature.
- **-10 box** (positions 23-29, TATAAT): perfect-consensus Pribnow box, *lac* UV5-derived.

The dual perfect-consensus boxes with optimal spacing make Ptrc one of the strongest constitutive sigma-70 promoter cores; the trailing G (position 29) is the start of the lac-UV5-derived downstream region. This part is the promoter core only — it does not include the +1 transcription start, the lac operator, or an RBS.

## Use
Ptrc drives high-level transcription in *E. coli* expression vectors such as pTrc99A and pKK233-2 (Amann 1985, [PMID 3007288](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3007288/); Amann 1988, [PMID 3069586](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3069586/)). In its standard vector context it is rendered IPTG-inducible by a downstream lac operator and the lacI^q repressor, both supplied by the host vector rather than this part.
