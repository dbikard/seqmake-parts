# T7_tag

N-terminal 11-residue epitope tag (MASMTGGQQMG) derived from bacteriophage T7 Gene product 10A (major capsid protein); widely used as a fusion tag for immunodetection and immunoaffinity purification of recombinant proteins.

## Origin

The sequence MASMTGGQQMG corresponds to residues 1–11 of T7 Gene product 10A (UniProt P19726, Swiss-Prot reviewed), the major capsid protein of *Escherichia* phage T7. Both the full-length 10A form (344 aa) and the frameshifted 10B variant (398 aa; [PMID:1938901](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1938901/), [PMID:1820210](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1820210/)) share this identical N-terminus. The tag entered recombinant-protein practice when T7-based expression systems incorporated the first ~40 residues of gene 10 as a translational-initiation leader upstream of cloned genes, thereby fusing the 11-aa tag to the N-terminus of the expressed protein ([PMID:3537305](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3537305/), [PMID:2199796](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2199796/)). Because the tag is non-self (bacteriophage-derived), anti-T7 monoclonal antibodies provide high specificity against it in cellular backgrounds.

## Properties

- **Sequence**: MASMTGGQQMG (11 aa; MW ~1.1 kDa)
- **Source organism**: *Escherichia* phage T7 (gene 10, gp10A/10B shared N-terminus)
- **Detection**: Recognised by anti-T7 monoclonal antibodies; compatible with Western blot, ELISA, immunoprecipitation, and immunofluorescence applications ([PMID:8840785](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8840785/))
- **Purification**: Can be used for immunoaffinity purification; elution typically requires low pH, which may denature acid-sensitive proteins and limits matrix reusability ([PMID:8840785](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8840785/))
- **Size**: Small (11 aa) — minimal structural perturbation of fusion partner
- **Atomic part**: No functional sub-elements; the 11 residues act as an indivisible epitope unit

## Use

The tag is fused N-terminally (most commonly) or C-terminally to a protein of interest via genetic cloning. A short flexible linker is often interposed. The tag is organism-agnostic: it has been validated in *E. coli* and *S. cerevisiae* ([PMID:9591127](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9591127/)), and broader eukaryotic applicability is widely assumed given its small, non-self character. Note that T7_tag (this record, protein sequence) is conceptually distinct from the upstream DNA element T7_g10_leader (the 5' UTR Shine-Dalgarno/RBS; a separate catalog part).
