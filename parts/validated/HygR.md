HygR (hph / APH(4)-Ia) is the *E. coli* hygromycin B 4-O-phosphotransferase that confers hygromycin B resistance by ATP/GTP-dependent phosphorylation of the antibiotic's cyclitol 4-OH.

## Origin
Encoded by the plasmid-borne *hph* gene first sequenced from *E. coli* by Gritz & Davies (1983, [PMID 6319235](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6319235/)), where it was shown to function as a dominant selectable marker in both *E. coli* and *S. cerevisiae* — the origin of the canonical "HygR" cassette used in many cloning and expression vectors. The 341-aa protein corresponds to UniProt [P00557](https://www.uniprot.org/uniprotkb/P00557) (KHYB_ECOLX), a reviewed Swiss-Prot entry with evidence at the protein level.

## Properties
HygR is an aminoglycoside-phosphotransferase-family enzyme, APH(4)-Ia, adopting the bilobal protein-kinase-like (ePK) fold (Stogios et al. 2011, [PMID 21084294](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21084294/)): an N-terminal beta-rich lobe (residues 7-92) and a larger alpha-helical C-terminal lobe (residues 99-301) joined by a hinge, with the active site in the interdomain cleft. Asp198, in the HADFGSNN catalytic loop, is the proton acceptor that activates the hygromycin B C4-hydroxyl for in-line phosphoryl transfer; Asp216 coordinates the catalytic Mg2+ and the nucleotide alpha-phosphate. The ternary complex with hygromycin B, an ATP analog (AMP-PNP), and two Mg2+ ions (PDB 3W0S) confirms domain closure on substrate binding and the catalytic importance of Asp198/Asn202/Asn203 (Iino et al. 2013, [PMID 23747390](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23747390/)).

## Use
Standard dominant selectable marker conferring resistance to hygromycin B in bacteria, yeast, plant, and mammalian systems. Phosphorylation at the 4-OH inactivates the drug, so HygR-expressing cells survive hygromycin B selection.
