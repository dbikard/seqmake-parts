ZeoR is the *Streptoalloteichus hindustanus* ble protein (UniProt P17493), a 124-aa bleomycin/Zeocin-resistance marker that confers resistance by stoichiometrically binding and sequestering the drug rather than degrading it.

## Origin
The gene (ble) comes from the actinomycete *Streptoalloteichus hindustanus*. The product was first shown to confer resistance by reversibly binding bleomycin 1:1, so the bound drug can no longer cleave DNA ([PMID 2450783](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2450783/)). The 2.3 A crystal structure and site-directed mutagenesis established the fold and the sequestering mechanism ([PMID 7516875](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7516875/)). The given sequence is identical to the canonical UniProt entry P17493 (length 124).

## Properties
- Single ~14 kDa chain that adopts the VOC / glyoxalase-bleomycin-resistance superfamily fold: a structure in two halves with the same alpha/beta fold despite no internal sequence similarity, spanning essentially the whole chain (residues 3-121, [PMID 7516875](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7516875/)).
- Functions as a homodimer assembled by mutual chain exchange; the ~8 N-terminal residues form the swapped arm that crosses to the partner subunit. One dimer sequesters two bleomycin molecules in a crevice at the dimer interface ([PMID 7516875](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7516875/)).
- Binding, not catalytic: there is no active site or metal cofactor; resistance is by drug sequestration.

## Use
Selectable marker conferring resistance to bleomycin-family antibiotics (bleomycin, phleomycin, Zeocin) in bacteria, yeast, and mammalian cells. An engineered thermostable variant extends its usable range to hyperthermophiles, illustrating its tractability as a marker scaffold ([PMID 15640151](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15640151/)).
