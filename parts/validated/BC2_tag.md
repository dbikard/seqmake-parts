# BC2_tag

BC2_tag is a 12-aa linear epitope tag (PDRKAAVSHWQQ) from the N-terminal region of human beta-catenin that is bound by the BC2 nanobody with low-nanomolar affinity for protein purification, immunodetection, and super-resolution imaging.

## Origin
BC2T is a natural linear epitope corresponding to residues 16–27 of human beta-catenin (UniProt P35222), repurposed as a minimal affinity/detection tag. It was defined together with its cognate single-domain antibody, the BC2 nanobody (BC2-Nb), which recognizes this short linear stretch rather than a conformational epitope (PMID 26791954).

## Properties
- Recognized by BC2-Nb with a dissociation constant of ~1.4 nM (surface plasmon resonance).
- Binds by an unusual "headlock" mode: the peptide adopts an extended beta-strand that becomes part of a beta-sheet in the nanobody, so most of the affinity comes from sequence-independent backbone hydrogen bonds, and a nanobody salt bridge clamps the bound peptide. Crystal structures of the free nanobody (2.0 A) and of the nanobody/peptide complex (1.0 A) were solved.
- Specificity is set by only four side chains — R3, A6, S8 and W10, with W10 invariable and most critical — while the other eight positions tolerate substitution, so the tag can be re-engineered (e.g. charge changes, added tryptophan for A280 readout, or a cleavage site) without losing binding.
- Recognized equally well at the N- or C-terminus; an N-terminal tag can be partially removed by proteolysis during lysis.
- Because the sequence comes from beta-catenin, phosphorylation of the residue at tag position 8 (Ser23 of beta-catenin) disfavors binding, so the nanobody prefers the non-/hypo-phosphorylated form — relevant mainly when tagging endogenous beta-catenin.

## Use
- One-step affinity purification on a BC2-Nb matrix, with gentle competitive elution by free BC2 peptide that releases natively folded protein (~60–80% recovery at 0.1–1 mM peptide). Capture is robust to harsh conditions (2% SDS, 4 M urea, up to 1.5 M GdmCl).
- Direct Western-blot detection and immunofluorescence using dye-labeled BC2-Nb, with no secondary antibody; the small epitope and small binder minimize steric hindrance and linkage error.
- Dense, low-linkage-error labeling for dSTORM super-resolution imaging via a bivalent BC2-Nb, in mammalian and yeast cells including endogenous CRISPR-tagged loci (PMID 29500346).
- An engineered, re-surfaced variant, Spot-tag (PDRVRAVSHWSS; four substitutions), is recognized by the same nanobody lineage and is held as a separate catalog part. Affinity can also be tuned on the binder side (e.g. nanobody variant 44D, ~100-fold lower affinity) to enable mild elution at high recovery.
