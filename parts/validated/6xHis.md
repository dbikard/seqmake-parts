# 6xHis

6xHis is the canonical hexahistidine (polyhistidine) affinity tag — a synthetic 6-residue
peptide (HHHHHH) used for single-step immobilized-metal affinity chromatography (IMAC)
purification of recombinant proteins.

## Origin

The 6xHis tag derives from the discovery that proteins and peptides bearing neighbouring
histidine residues bind selectively to immobilized divalent transition-metal ions on a
nitrilotriacetic-acid (NTA) chelate matrix (Hochuli et al. 1987). The following year this
was turned into a general purification strategy: a short polyhistidine peptide genetically
fused to a recombinant protein renders the fusion purifiable by IMAC (Hochuli et al.
1988). The tag is purely synthetic and has no standalone natural protein of origin; the
canonical 6-aa sequence is deposited as the bound antigen in the crystal structure of the
anti-His-tag antibody 3D5 scFv (PDB 1KTR, Kaufmann et al. 2002).

## Properties

- Sequence: HHHHHH (6 residues), molecule type protein.
- Mechanism: adjacent histidine imidazole side chains chelate immobilized Ni²⁺, Co²⁺, Cu²⁺
  or Zn²⁺ ions on an IMAC matrix (NTA or IDA); the tagged protein is recovered by imidazole
  competition or low-pH elution.
- Small and minimally perturbing; functions across expression organisms and under both
  native and denaturing conditions (e.g. 6–8 M urea or guanidine), since binding does not
  require a folded epitope.
- Detectable with anti-polyhistidine antibodies.
- Length variants of the polyhistidine tag (8xHis, 10xHis) are also used; the 6xHis form is
  the canonical minimal tag.

## Use

Fused at the N- or C-terminus of a protein coding sequence to enable affinity capture and
purification by IMAC. Because it tolerates denaturing buffers, it is commonly used to purify
both soluble and insoluble (inclusion-body) proteins. Reviews of its mechanism, metal-ion
and matrix choices, placement, detection and removal: Terpe 2003 and Kimple et al. 2013.
