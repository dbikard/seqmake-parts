# NusA

E. coli K-12 NusA (N-utilization substance protein A) is an essential, single-copy transcription elongation factor (495 aa, ~54 kDa) that binds the RNA polymerase core enzyme during elongation and stimulates both transcriptional pausing and intrinsic (Rho-independent) termination. It is also widely deployed as an N-terminal solubility-enhancing fusion tag for recombinant protein production.

## Origin

NusA is encoded by the *nusA* gene of *Escherichia coli* K-12 (UniProt P0AFF6, Swiss-Prot reviewed, locus b3996). It was originally identified as a factor required for bacteriophage lambda N-mediated antitermination and was shown to bind RNAP core equimolarly while being displaced by sigma factor at initiation (PMID 6263495).

## Properties

NusA is a multi-domain protein with the following experimentally defined architecture (reviewed in PMID 21922055):

- **NTD (aa 1–137):** Interacts with the RNAP beta-flap tip helix at the RNA exit channel. The NTD alone is necessary and sufficient for pausing enhancement (PMID 20600118, truncation series).
- **S1 and KH domains:** RNA-binding modules that contact the nascent transcript.
- **AR1:** Acidic repeat 1; directly contacts the bacteriophage lambda N antiterminator peptide.
- **AR2:** Acidic repeat 2; autoinhibitory — it occludes AR1 and modulates NusA activity.

NusA acts as a passive holdase when used as a fusion tag: it prevents aggregation of the fused partner co-translationally but does not actively refold misfolded proteins (PMID 16168669; mechanism confirmed and generalized in PMID 24942647 comparing NusA and MBP). Typical fusion protein yields are 13–20 mg/L in *E. coli* (PMID 15336530). At temperatures above 45 °C, NusA forms high-molecular-weight oligomers via its C-terminal AR domain and acquires chaperone/holdase activity (PMID 23907089).

## Use

NusA is used in two distinct contexts:

1. **Transcription biology reagent:** Full-length NusA is used to study pausing, termination, antitermination, and transcription–translation coupling in *E. coli* and related organisms.

2. **Solubility-enhancing fusion tag:** Fused N-terminally to difficult-to-express recombinant proteins; frequently combined with a C-terminal His6 tag for two-step IMAC purification (PMID 17406446). Removal of NusA is achieved by interposing a protease recognition site between NusA and the passenger protein.
