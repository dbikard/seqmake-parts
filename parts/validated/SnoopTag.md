# SnoopTag

SnoopTag is a 12-residue reactive peptide (KLGDIEFIKVNK) that forms a spontaneous covalent isopeptide bond with its partner protein SnoopCatcher and is orthogonal to the SpyTag/SpyCatcher system.

## Origin
SnoopTag was engineered by splitting the D4 (CnaA-like) domain of RrgA, the pilus adhesin of *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (UniProt A7KT94), into a small reactive peptide (SnoopTag) and a larger partner protein (SnoopCatcher). The peptide corresponds to RrgA residues 727–738. The sequence was verified at 100% identity over all 12 residues against the GenBank deposit AMD39968.1 ("SnoopTag-MBP", 10-FEB-2016) accompanying the defining publication.

## Properties
- **Reaction:** On mixing, SnoopTag and SnoopCatcher reconstitute the split RrgA domain and form a spontaneous, covalent, irreversible isopeptide (amide) bond between a lysine side chain on the tag and an asparagine side chain on the partner. The exact reactive-residue assignments rest on data in the primary figures that were not accessible for this record.
- **Yield:** The pair reacts to >99% completion.
- **Orthogonality:** SnoopTag/SnoopCatcher react only with each other and do not cross-react with SpyTag/SpyCatcher, allowing the two superglue pairs to be used together.
- **Size/format:** A short, genetically encodable peptide that can be fused at either terminus or in loops of a protein of interest.

## Use
SnoopTag enables programmable, irreversible covalent conjugation of protein modules. Fused to one partner and reacted with SnoopCatcher fused to another, it covalently joins the two. Combined orthogonally with SpyTag/SpyCatcher, it supports sequential and combinatorial assembly of linear, branched, and combinatorial polyprotein chains ("polyproteams"), and plug-and-display attachment of antigens or ligands onto multivalent scaffolds. The cognate partner SnoopCatcher is required for any application. Related RrgA-derived ligation tools (SnoopTagJr, DogTag, SnoopTag2) extend the same scaffold with distinct sequences and partners.
