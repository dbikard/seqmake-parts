Tn5 aminoglycoside 3'-phosphotransferase type II (APH(3')-II / NPTII), a 264-aa kinase-fold enzyme that confers kanamycin and neomycin resistance by ATP-dependent phosphorylation of the aminoglycoside 3'-OH.

## Origin
The gene (neo / nptII / kan) was sequenced from transposon Tn5 by Beck et al. 1982, who localized the ORF and defined the 264-residue product. The protein sequence is an exact match to UniProt P00552 (KKA2_KLEPN, *Klebsiella pneumoniae* neo). It is the canonical selectable marker behind the common "KanR"/"NeoR" cassettes.

## Properties
APH(3')-II adopts a two-lobe, protein-kinase-like fold (crystal structure of APH(3')-IIa with kanamycin, PDB 1ND4; Nurizzo et al. 2003). Catalysis proceeds through **Asp190**, the catalytic base that deprotonates the kanamycin 3'-OH for in-line attack on the ATP gamma-phosphate (confirmed by QM/MM on the homologous APH(3')-IIIa). Asp190 sits in the conserved HGDxxxxN catalytic loop (His188-Asn195); **Asn195** and **Asp208** (in the IDCG / DFG-analogous motif) coordinate the catalytic Mg2+. Aminoglycoside recognition is driven by a highly acidic cleft: the **acidic loop residues 151-166** and the **C-terminal residues 260-264** contact the three kanamycin rings.

## Use
Standard kanamycin/neomycin selection marker for bacterial (and, as NPTII, eukaryotic) constructs. Encoded as the protein; codon-optimize for the host before cloning. The part is the protein only — the native stop codon and any promoter (e.g. a separate Pkan/neo promoter part) lie outside this feature.
