# CBP_tag

CBP_tag is a 26-aa calmodulin-binding peptide from the C-terminus of myosin light-chain kinase that binds calmodulin in a Ca2+-dependent manner and is released with EGTA, serving as a reversible affinity handle.

## Origin
The tag corresponds to residues 578-603 of rabbit skeletal/cardiac myosin light-chain kinase (UniProt P07313), the C-terminal calmodulin-binding domain first defined as a 27-residue fragment that inhibits calmodulin activation of the kinase (Ki ~1 nM) and binds calmodulin Ca2+-dependently with 1:1 stoichiometry ([PMID 3858814](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3858814/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.82.10.3187)). The peptide-calmodulin interaction was further characterized spectroscopically ([PMID 3841496](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3841496/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1021/bi00348a047)). The sequence is conserved identically across mammalian MLCK orthologs.

## Properties
- Binds calmodulin with nanomolar affinity in the presence of Ca2+; complex dissociates upon Ca2+ chelation ([PMID 9047344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9047344/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0378-1119(96)00680-4)).
- A UniProt-annotated calmodulin-binding core (MLCK residues 586-598; part positions 8-21) lies within the peptide.
- An engineered N5A point variant (RWKKNFIAVSAANRFKKIS) binds calmodulin with ultra-high affinity (Kd ~2 pM), reversible with EDTA ([PMID 26159704](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26159704/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jmb.2015.06.018)).
- The peptide can be phosphorylated by cAMP-dependent protein kinase, enabling direct 32P labeling of fusion proteins ([PMID 9047344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9047344/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0378-1119(96)00680-4)).

## Use
- One-step purification of fusion proteins on immobilized calmodulin, with EGTA elution under mild conditions; first introduced as part of a DNA cassette carrying a kinase phosphorylation site, a Factor Xa site, and the calmodulin-binding peptide ([PMID 1318232](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1318232/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/0014-5793(92)80458-s)).
- N-terminal fusion supports efficient translation and high-level expression in E. coli; the tag is removable by a protease site (Factor Xa or thrombin) supplied between the passenger and the peptide ([PMID 9047344](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9047344/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0378-1119(96)00680-4)).
- Serves as the second affinity handle in tandem-affinity purification of native protein complexes (TAP and related tandem tags) ([PMID 10504710](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10504710/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/13732); [PMID 33654794](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33654794/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3791/61937)).
- Practical purification conditions for calmodulin-binding-peptide fusions are reviewed in ([PMID 11036652](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11036652/), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/s0076-6879(00)26062-8)).
