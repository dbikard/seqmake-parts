# TEV_site

TEV_site is the engineered ENLYFQG (Glu-Asn-Leu-Tyr-Phe-Gln-Gly) heptapeptide recognized by Tobacco Etch Virus (TEV) NIa protease, which cleaves between the P1 Gln and the P1' Gly (ENLYFQ|G) — a highly specific, minimal-scar site for releasing a passenger protein from its fusion tag.

## Origin
The TEV recognition site derives from the seven proteolytic processing junctions of the TEV genome polyprotein, where the NIa protease autocatalytically cleaves a conserved E-X-X-Y-X-Q-(S/G) motif. Site-directed mutagenesis of the polyprotein cleavage site first defined the 7-residue ENLYFQ(G/S) heptapeptide and the strict conservation requirements at each position (PMID 3409865), and a companion study identified the consensus motif shared across the polyprotein junctions (PMID 3285343). The natural NIb|CP junction sequence is ENLYFQS, with serine at the P1' position. The sequence stored here, ENLYFQG, is the engineered P1'-Gly variant: it differs from the natural substrate only at position 7 (Ser->Gly), is fully accepted by TEV protease, and leaves a single non-native glycine on the released downstream product (PMID 12074568). Both the Ser and Gly forms are established recombinant TEV sites.

## Properties
TEV protease (EC 3.4.22.44), a cysteine protease, recognizes the 7-residue motif Glu-Asn-Leu-Tyr-Phe-Gln-(Gly/Ser) spanning positions P6 through P1', and hydrolyzes the scissile bond between the P1 glutamine and the P1' residue (ENLYFQ|G). The crystal structure of the protease bound to a substrate/product peptide explains the structural basis of this heptapeptide selectivity and confirms the cut between Q and the P1' residue (PMID 12377789). The P1' position tolerates several residues — Gly, Ser, Ala, Met, Cys, His — but not proline; the residue placed at P1' becomes the new N-terminal residue of the released product, so the ENLYFQG form leaves an N-terminal glycine while the natural ENLYFQS form leaves an N-terminal serine (PMID 12074568). The 7-residue recognition requirement makes the site highly sequence-specific, giving clean, minimal-scar tag removal. The cognate enzyme is most often used as the autolysis-resistant S219V recombinant variant, which retains wild-type catalytic proficiency while resisting self-cleavage (PMID 11809930).

## Use
The element is placed in the linker between an affinity/solubility tag and the target protein so that, after capture and purification, the tag is removed by adding TEV protease — performed in vitro or by co-expression in vivo. Cleavage releases the passenger with at most a single residual N-terminal residue (the P1' Gly in this form), and the high specificity of the 7-residue site minimizes off-target cleavage. Because the protease is supplied separately, the site itself is host-agnostic. The founding demonstration used a recombinant plant-virus proteinase to release proteins and peptides from fusion proteins (PMID 8179197). The TEV site is one of a family of protease cleavage sites (alongside Factor Xa, thrombin, enterokinase, and HRV3C sites) used for tag removal; the choice of P1' residue (Gly vs Ser) determines the single scar residue left on the product.
