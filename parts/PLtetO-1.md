PLtetO-1 is the synthetic TetR-repressible / aTc-inducible promoter of Lutz & Bujard (1997), built by inserting two Tn10 tetO2 operators into the bacteriophage lambda PL scaffold.

## Origin
PLtetO-1 was designed by Lutz & Bujard ([PMID 9092630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9092630/)) by chemical synthesis on the bacteriophage lambda PL promoter scaffold. The lambda cI operator sites were replaced with two copies of the 19 bp Tn10 tetO2 operator (consensus TCCCTATCAGTGATAGAGA, [PMID 2835082](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2835082/)): one upstream of the -35 hexamer and one inserted into the -35/-10 spacer.

## Properties
The promoter uses the lambda PL sigma-70 elements: a canonical -35 (TTGACA) and a non-consensus -10 (GATACT) separated by a canonical 17 bp spacer. The two tetO2 operators flank the -35 and occupy the spacer (overlapping the -10 by 2 bp). In the absence of inducer, the TetR dimer ([PMID 8153629](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8153629/)) binds the operators and occludes RNA polymerase. Anhydrotetracycline (aTc) releases TetR, giving up to ~5000-fold induction. The first 54 bp match the canonical reference; the 3' tail (positions 48-73) carries the discriminator and transcription start region.

## Use
Used as a tightly regulatable expression promoter in E. coli, paired with a TetR source and induced by aTc. It is widely distributed in the iGEM Registry as BBa_R0040 ('pTet'). An RBS is supplied as a separate downstream module, not part of this promoter element.
