The Tn10 divergent tetA/tetR control region: 56 bp carrying the PtetA promoter, the overlapping PtetR promoter, and the two TetR operators tetO1 and tetO2.

## Origin
This fragment is the intergenic regulatory region of the Tn10 (class B) tetracycline-resistance determinant, where the divergent promoters for *tetA* (efflux pump) and *tetR* (repressor) overlap. The architecture was defined by Bertrand et al. 1983, who mapped the overlapping divergent promoters (transcription start sites ~36 bp apart) and the two dyad-symmetry operators ([PMID 6311683](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6311683/)). Daniels & Bertrand 1985 confirmed the -35/-10 assignments and the PtetR1/PtetR2 starts via promoter-down mutations ([PMID 2995683](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2995683/)).

## Properties
- **PtetA -35** (TTGACA, positions 1-7): canonical sigma70 -35 hexamer driving *tetA*.
- **PtetA -10** (TTATTT, positions 24-30): degenerate -10 hexamer, 17 bp downstream of the -35 (consensus spacer). It is degenerate because tetO1 is superimposed on the -10/spacer.
- **tetO1** (CACTCTATCATTGATAGAG, positions 5-24): 19 bp TetR operator overlapping the PtetA -10/spacer; chiefly controls PtetR; ~2x lower TetR affinity than O2.
- **tetO2** (TCCCTATCAGTGATAGAGA, positions 36-55): 19 bp non-palindromic, higher-affinity wild-type operator overlapping the divergent PtetR -10; mainly controls PtetA.

Both operators share the TGATAGAG core and bind TetR via its HTH domain; the TetR-operator interaction was solved structurally by Hinrichs et al. 1994 ([PMID 8153629](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8153629/)). Differential O1/O2 regulation, with little cooperativity, was shown by Meier et al. 1988 ([PMID 2835235](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2835235/)); operator base pairs contacting TetR were mapped by Wissmann et al. 1986 ([PMID 3086838](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3086838/)).

## Use
A tetracycline-inducible switch: bound TetR represses transcription from PtetA (and PtetR); tetracycline / anhydrotetracycline (aTc) releases TetR, de-repressing the promoters. It is the basis of Tet-OFF/Tet-inducible expression systems ([PMID 7826010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7826010/)). The defining caveat is overlap: editing a promoter hexamer also perturbs the operator on it, and vice versa. The cognate repressor TetR(B) and the inducer (aTc) are required for function but lie outside this fragment.
